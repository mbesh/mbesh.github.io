Feast at Beast

As with our relationship, we are kicking off our marriage with a hearty and celebratory feast.

Please join us on Sunday, October 14, 2018, for a feast at Beast, consisting of six courses including wine pairings.

Dinner service will begin promptly at 7:00 p.m., so please plan your transportation and parking accordingly.

The restaurant is located in Northeast Portland near the Alberta Arts District at the intersection of Killingsworth Street and 30th Avenue. We recommend TriMet or travelling by foot or bicycle for maximum joy, the environment, and ease of parking.

Beast is the creation of Chef Naomi Pomeroy, who was born and raised in Megan’s hometown of Corvallis, Oregon. To get an idea of the cuisine, check out a sample menu.

Playing in Portlandia

It is impossible to talk about Portland without mentioning all of the amazing food, coffee, beer, and wine prepared with ingredients proudly and lovingly grown in the Pacific Northwest.  Although everyone should enjoy a spooky treat from Voodoo Doughnuts at some point in their life, Megan insists the best donuts are at Blue Star Donuts.  When it’s time for lunch, don’t miss the amazing falafels from Wolf & Bear’s or choose your own adventure by stopping by one of the many pods of food trucks the city is famous for.  Pick a coffee shop at random and you are sure to be impressed, we especially recommend Heart Coffee Roasters and Barista as well as Townshend’s Tea (try the Kashmiri Chai or Matejuana, which does not actually contain marijuana).

Fortunately, Portland is also an extremely walkable city, so you can easily atone for your gluttony in between stops.  Get some stair climbing in while you peruse the many floors of books at Powell’s City of Books.  Take advantage of the many miles of trails in Forest Park, Washington Park, or Mt. Tabor Park or stroll the many corridors of the Portland Art Museum or the Oregon Museum of Science and Industry (which can now be accessed on foot via the Tilikum Bridge).

Once you have gotten your steps in, quench your thirst at Belmont Station, Hair of the Dog Brewery, or Base Camp Brewing.  Although Deschutes is a classic, you can get it at the store, so Megan recommends checking out the brewery launched by one of its former brewmasters instead: Ecliptic Brewing.  If beer is not your thing, grab a drink at Clyde Common or cool off in the subterranean wine tasting room at Les Caves.

how we met

Mike and Megan first crossed paths at a housewarming barbecue in Santa Clara, California.  Megan had moved just a few streets away from a townhouse, the Roost, to a house full of surfing Canadians.  After a morning of surfing in nearby Santa Cruz, Megan’s housemates hosted a housewarming barbecue, inviting their friend Mike who had just moved to the Bay Area from Toronto.

Yay Area Romance

Over the course of many shared meals and adventures, from their first date at a ramen shop to the countless Clif bars they split on the chairlift, their mutual love, respect, and admiration for each other grew.  In between eating tacos al pastor at Metro Balderas and skiing, Megan and Mike supported each other through job changes, moves, sports injuries, and an addiction to spreadsheets and organizational software (Megan’s).
